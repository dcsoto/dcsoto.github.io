My current research focuses on studying previously unexplored complex human variation—namely structural variation and duplicated regions—using a combination of both short- and long-read sequencing technologies to answer questions pertaining to the evolution of our uniquely human features and diseases.

More generally, I'm interested in primate's complex genomic variation, including (i) improving discovery and fixing reference genomes, and (ii) assessing its functional, evolutionary, and phenotypic impact.
