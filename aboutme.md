I am a senior PhD candidate with a background in biochemical engineering and over six years of experience in the fields of computational biology and genomics. I am currently finishing my PhD dissertation at UC Davis in the [Dennis Lab](https://www.dennislab.org/), where I study complex genomic variation in great apes and its role in primate-specific traits and diseases, leveraging long-read sequencing technologies and multi-omics data. Previously, I worked as a bioinformatics research assistant at Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile in plant transcriptomics.

I'm passionate about long-read sequencing, structural variation, great apes evolution, omics integration, and reproducible bioinformatics. You can learn more about my research here.

I've been into digital drawing for several years and I'm open to opportunities to illustrate science-related topics and graphic design for conferences/symposiums. You can check my portfolio here.

I was born in Santiago, Chile. I would love to meet over coffee/tea to talk about genomics, bioinformatics, or Avatar The Last Airbender.
